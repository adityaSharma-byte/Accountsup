Overview

Provincial Sales Tax for Canada

Indirect tax has quietly become one of the most error-prone areas of finance. Since the 2018 Wayfair ruling, online sales alone can create economic nexus in a state you have never set foot in — and every jurisdiction sets its own thresholds, rates, filing calendars, and product-taxability rules. AccountsUp gives you a dedicated team that tracks all of it and files on time, every period.

Whether you owe US sales tax across forty-five states, UK VAT under Making Tax Digital, Canadian GST/HST and provincial PST, or Australian GST reported on a BAS, we work inside your existing stack — QuickBooks, Xero, NetSuite, Sage, and tax engines like Avalara or TaxJar — so returns reconcile back to the ledger and nothing is filed from a spreadsheet guess.

When a state notice or audit does arrive, you are not scrambling for records. Every return we file is backed by reconciled workpapers, exemption-certificate documentation, and a clear liability trail, so responding to an auditor is a matter of retrieving evidence we already organized — not reconstructing three years of transactions under a deadline.

  • Full compliance across every state, province, and country you sell into
  • A clean monthly reconciliation of tax collected, remitted, and reported
  • Audit-ready workpapers and certificates that turn notices into quick, low-stress responses
  • Reclaimed finance-team hours and fewer penalty and interest surprises

Questions

Provincial Sales Tax, answered

How do I know if I have sales-tax nexus in a state I don't operate in?

Since the 2018 Wayfair decision, most states impose economic nexus once your sales exceed a threshold — commonly $100,000 in revenue or 200 transactions per year, though it varies. Our nexus study measures your activity against each state's current rule and tells you exactly where you must register.

What if I've already missed registrations and owe back tax?

This is common and fixable. Rather than registering and inviting questions about prior periods, we often negotiate a voluntary disclosure agreement, which typically limits the look-back period and waives or reduces penalties. We quantify the exposure first, then recommend the lowest-cost path to becoming compliant.

Do you work in our existing accounting and tax software?

Yes. We work directly inside QuickBooks, Xero, NetSuite, and Sage, and configure and run tax engines like Avalara and TaxJar. There is no migration and no separate portal — we integrate with the stack you already use, so returns always reconcile back to your ledger.

How is my financial data kept secure?

Access is role-based and protected by multi-factor authentication, with every action captured in an audit trail. We operate under SOC 2-aligned controls and GDPR-compliant data handling, work from secured offices rather than personal devices, and never move your data outside approved, encrypted systems.

How much does it cost?

Pricing depends on jurisdiction count, transaction volume, and filing frequency. Most engagements start with a fixed monthly fee covering a set number of returns, plus one-time project fees for nexus studies or registrations. After a short scoping call we provide a transparent, itemized quote with no long-term lock-in.

How long does onboarding take?

Most clients are filing with us within two to four weeks. We begin with a nexus and exposure review, then handle registrations and configure your tax engine in parallel. Businesses already registered and reconciled can often be ready for the next filing period.
