Overview

Finance & Accounting Consulting

Finance & Accounting Consulting from AccountsUp rebuilds the machinery behind your numbers. Instead of patching a broken close or another failed software rollout, we map your order-to-cash, procure-to-pay, and record-to-report flows end to end, then redesign them around clear controls, defined ownership, and the tools you already run. The result is a finance function that produces reliable numbers on a predictable calendar — not one that depends on a single person's spreadsheet and memory.

Every engagement pairs a dedicated consulting team with AI-assisted analysis that reads your ledgers, reconciliations, and transaction history to surface where time, cash, and accuracy actually leak. We work inside your existing stack — QuickBooks, Xero, NetSuite, Sage, ADP — so recommendations are grounded in how your business really operates, not a generic maturity model. You get a documented target state, a sequenced roadmap, and hands-on support through the change, not a slide deck that gathers dust.

Whether you are a CPA firm standardizing delivery across dozens of clients or a scaling company outgrowing its founder-era bookkeeping, we meet you where you are. From a single process redesign to a full finance transformation with a new ERP at its center, the work is scoped, priced, and staffed to the outcome you need — with knowledge transfer built in so your team owns the improved process long after we step back.

  • A faster, more predictable month-end close on a calendar the whole team trusts
  • Management reports leadership can actually decide on, with a defined source behind every number
  • A finance system that fits how you operate, adopted and used instead of worked around
  • Documented processes and controls your team owns and runs confidently on its own

Questions

Finance & Accounting Consulting, answered

What does a finance and accounting consulting engagement actually cover?

It spans three connected areas: process design (redesigning your close, AP/AR, and reporting workflows with SOPs and controls), system selection (choosing and configuring the right accounting platform), and finance transformation (sequencing both into a phased roadmap). We scope each engagement to the outcome you need, from a single process fix to a full ERP-centered rebuild.

Do I have to switch accounting software to work with you?

No. Our system evaluation is vendor-neutral, and in many cases the right answer is to configure your current platform better rather than replace it. When a migration genuinely serves you, we say so and prove it with a requirements matrix and total-cost model. We work fluently inside QuickBooks, Xero, NetSuite, Sage, and ADP either way.

How long does a typical consulting engagement take?

A focused process assessment or system selection usually runs a few weeks; a full finance transformation with implementation spans several months across phases. We define the timeline up front in the roadmap, tied to defined milestones, so you always know what is happening in each phase and when the next deliverable lands.

How do you keep our financial data secure during the engagement?

We operate under enterprise-grade controls: role-based access limited to your assigned team, multi-factor authentication, encrypted data handling, audit trails on activity, and secure office-based operations. Access is provisioned to the minimum needed and revoked at close. We can also work within your own environment and align to your firm's security policies.

Will my team be able to run the new process after you leave?

Yes — that is the point. Every engagement produces written SOPs, RACI ownership maps, and hands-on training, and knowledge transfer is built into the plan rather than bolted on. We deliberately avoid creating dependency on us; success is your team operating the improved close and reporting confidently on their own.

How is this different from just hiring a bookkeeping or accounting service?

Bookkeeping runs your existing process; consulting redesigns it. We diagnose why the close is slow, the numbers are unreliable, or the software fights you, then rebuild the workflow, controls, and system underneath. Many clients pair the two — we fix the machinery, then ongoing services operate it — but the consulting work is about changing how finance runs, not just doing the work.
